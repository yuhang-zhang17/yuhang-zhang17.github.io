## Research Focus

My research centers on the modeling, optimization, and control of district-scale energy systems, with emphasis on scalable, data-informed solutions for low-carbon heating and cooling infrastructure.

Core themes include:

- District heating and cooling networks  
- Thermal microgrids and electrification  
- Model predictive control (MPC)  
- Physics-based + data-driven hybrid modeling  
- System-level techno-economic optimization  

---

## Funded Research Projects

### AI-Empowered District Heat Pump Systems (NSF PIRE)

**Role:** Graduate Research Assistant  
**Focus:** Modeling and advanced control of district-scale heat pump systems  

- Developed system-level dynamic models for integrated heat pump networks  
- Designed model predictive control strategies to improve operational efficiency  
- Integrated physics-based and data-driven approaches for performance optimization  
- Evaluated scalability and decarbonization potential at district scale  

---

### Thermal Microgrid Feasibility & Optimization (DoD ESTCP)

**Focus:** District-scale energy planning and operational optimization  

- Built techno-economic optimization models for thermal microgrids  
- Assessed feasibility under varying load, climate, and infrastructure constraints  
- Quantified energy efficiency and lifecycle cost impacts  

---

### Solar-Geothermal District Energy Systems (DOE Sol-GDHC)

**Focus:** Integrated renewable district heating and cooling  

- Modeled hybrid solar-geothermal thermal networks  
- Performed feasibility and performance assessment across system configurations  
- Analyzed integration strategies for large-scale electrified heating systems
